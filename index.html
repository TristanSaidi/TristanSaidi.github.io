Tristan Luca Saidi

I am an incoming PhD student in the department of Statistics and Data Science at Carnegie Mellon University. Before that I completed my first year of graduate studies at Columbia University in the department of Computer Science, where I was very fortunate to work with Professor Andrew J. Blumberg and Professor Matei Ciocarlie. During this time, I worked on theoretically grounded methods that use discrete graph curvature to improve geometric data analysis. Prior to that, I worked primarily on robot learning for dexterous manipulation, and spent some time working on reinforcement learning for snake locomotion as well.

Research

I'm interested in developing theoretically justified approaches for recovering the geometric structure of real world data. Primarily, I'm interested in improving manifold learning techniques for the benefit of biologists working with single-cell RNA sequencing data.
